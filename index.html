ABOUT ME

Hi, I'm Dashiell! I'm a genome biologist with interests in DNA replication, recombination, and repair. More broadly, I'm interested in what we can learn from standing variation, loosely construed: while we often study variation in order to learn about disease and pathogenesis, I am fascinated by uncovering what the variation that is tolerated can teach us about the maintenance of healthy homeostasis.

In addition to my research interests, I am passionate about teaching, science literacy, and promoting equity in STEM — goals that I see as deeply intertwined with one another (and with my research interests).

I am currently a Ph.D. candidate in the lab of Dr. Amnon Koren in the Department of Molecular Biology and Genetics at Cornell University, and a student in the Graduate Field of Genetics, Genomics, and Development.

In the Koren lab, we study DNA replication timing in humans. My dissertation research focuses on uncovering heterogeneity in replication timing at the single-cell level, in order to better understand the regulation of this phenomenon. Read about it on Biorxiv !
